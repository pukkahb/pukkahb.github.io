About Me

I am a passionate and results-oriented professional transitioning from a strong background in zoology and public health into the dynamic world of bioinformatics and data science. My journey began with a focus on understanding pathogens and disease transmission at a microscopic level. However, I soon realised the immense potential of computational analysis to unlock patterns and insights from vast biological datasets.

This realisation drove me to acquire skills in programming languages like Python and R, data manipulation libraries such as Pandas, and machine learning frameworks like Scikit-learn. I am adept at transforming raw data into meaningful visualisations and predictive models that can inform public health strategies and clinical research. My unique interdisciplinary perspective allows me to bridge the gap between wet-lab research and computational analysis, ensuring that data-driven solutions are both technically sound and biologically relevant. I am eager to apply my skills to solve real-world health problems and contribute to a healthier future for all.
